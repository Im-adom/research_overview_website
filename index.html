01 ‚Äî The Problem

Why Current Dietary Science Is Failing Us

The Measurement Crisis What people eat is the most important dietary variable ‚Äî and the hardest to measure accurately. Social pressure, memory failure, and interview dynamics all conspire to corrupt the data.
10‚Äì50%
Systematic Under-Reporting
Participants selectively omit "unhealthy" foods when reporting to human interviewers. In obese populations, reported intake deviates from measured intake by 40‚Äì50%. This is structured, directional bias that invalidates downstream inference.
3 core methods
Flawed Assessment Tools
24-hour recalls, food diaries, and FFQs each carry known structural limitations. Even the gold-standard USDA Automated Multiple-Pass Method cannot eliminate social desirability bias when a human interviewer is present.
~Zero
Scalable Personalization
Generic guidelines (MyPlate, DASH) produce low adherence. Dietitian-led personalization is effective but unscalable. Current AI tools hallucinate nutritional facts and lack grounding in peer-reviewed evidence.
02 ‚Äî Methodological Context

The Foundational Limitation of Nutrition Research

Every major dietary assessment method carries documented, persistent limitations that undermine both research validity and clinical utility. The root cause is consistent: human judgment contaminates the measurement when participants perceive social evaluation.

Assessment Method Primary Structural Limitation Bias Classification
24-Hour Dietary Recall Memory-dependent; significant interviewer effect on reporting completeness Social desirability
Food Diary / Prospective Record High participant burden; reactive dietary modification during the recording period Reactivity bias
Food Frequency Questionnaire Cognitive overload; systematic portion estimation error across food categories Recall bias
USDA AMPM (Gold Standard) Cannot resolve social desirability bias inherent to human-administered recall Persistent bias
03 ‚Äî Research Objectives

What This Research Sets Out to Do

Three integrated aims address both the data quality problem and the personalization gap ‚Äî progressing from tool development through validation to a full mixed-methods pilot evaluation of the complete integrated system.

01
Conversational Agent Development & Validation
Adapt and extend Intake24 with a voice-enabled conversational agent embodied as a food character. Evaluate its effect on dietary recall completeness and perceived social judgment versus standard Intake24. Primary outcome: measurable reduction in social desirability bias via validated instruments and biomarker comparison.
02
RAG System Construction & Validation
Build and validate a Retrieval-Augmented Generation system integrating ABCD user data, USDA FoodData Central, Open Food Facts, TheMealDB, ProteinNAP, and vectorized PubMed literature for generating personalized 7-day cyclic meal plans. Evaluate accuracy, citation quality, and hallucination rate against expert comparator plans.
03
Integrated System Pilot Evaluation
Conduct a mixed-methods pilot study evaluating the complete integrated system end-to-end. Outcomes include: System Usability Scale scores, perceived judgment measures, meal plan adherence at 2 and 4 weeks, relevant dietary biomarkers, and qualitative experience via structured interviews.
04 ‚Äî Methodology

The Research Workflow in Detail

A Two-Phase End-to-End System Phase 1 captures debiased dietary data via a voice-enabled conversational agent. Phase 2 converts that data into evidence-grounded personalized meal plans using a RAG model hosted on Open Scholar.

The methodology is structured as two connected phases. The D component of ABCD data (Dietary intake) is captured in Phase 1 through an adapted Intake24 platform with a novel voice interface. The full ABCD profile ‚Äî Anthropometric, Biochemical, Clinical, and Dietary data ‚Äî then feeds Phase 2, where a RAG model generates personalized, evidence-based cyclic meal plans.

Every output is grounded in peer-reviewed evidence retrieved from a curated knowledge base, ensuring recommendations are traceable, accurate, and free from AI hallucination.
